Dining in Taniti

From fresh seafood to international cuisine

Restaurants

Taniti offers 10 restaurants ranging from local flavors to familiar favorites

Local Tanitian Cuisine

Experience authentic Tanitian flavors featuring the freshest local ingredients. Our traditional cuisine centers around the bounty of the sea and the island's rich agricultural heritage.

  • Fresh-caught fish prepared daily
  • Locally-grown rice dishes
  • Tropical fruits and vegetables
  • Traditional cooking methods passed down through generations

Must Try: Ask for the daily catch—you won't find fresher fish anywhere!

American-Style Restaurants

Missing familiar flavors? Several restaurants in Taniti City offer American-style cuisine for travelers seeking comfort food.

  • Burgers, steaks, and classic American dishes
  • Pizza and pasta options
  • Family-friendly menus with kids' options
  • Sports bars with satellite TV

Pan-Asian Cuisine

Our Pan-Asian restaurants offer a fusion of flavors from across Asia, prepared with fresh local ingredients.

  • Chinese, Japanese, and Thai influences
  • Fresh sushi and sashimi
  • Vegetarian and vegan options available
  • Unique fusion dishes blending Asian and island flavors

Drinks & Nightlife

Enjoy tropical cocktails and local brews

Bars & Nightlife

Merriton Landing is the heart of Taniti's nightlife scene, offering everything from casual beach bars to lively dance clubs.

  • Local microbrewery with craft beers
  • Beachfront bars with sunset views
  • Dance club open Thursday through Saturday
  • Live music at various venues
  • Tropical cocktails featuring local fruits

Note: The drinking age on Taniti is 18. Please drink responsibly.

Grocery & Self-Catering

Options for those who prefer to cook their own meals

Grocery Stores

Taniti has two grocery stores for those who prefer to prepare their own meals or need supplies during their stay.

  • One larger supermarket in Taniti City
  • One smaller convenience store near Merriton Landing
  • Fresh produce, meats, and seafood available
  • International products and familiar brands stocked
  • Many accommodations have kitchen facilities

Dining Tips

Reservations: While most restaurants don't require reservations, it's recommended for resort dining and popular spots on weekends.

Hours: Many local restaurants close between 2-5 PM for afternoon rest. Plan accordingly!

Tipping: Tipping is appreciated but not required. 10-15% is customary for exceptional service.
