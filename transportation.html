Getting To & Around Taniti

Everything you need to know about transportation

Getting to Taniti

Two ways to reach our island paradise

By Air

Taniti is served by a small airport that can accommodate small jets and propeller planes. Most visitors arrive via connecting flights from larger regional airports.

  • Small airport with modern facilities
  • Currently accommodates small jets and prop planes
  • Runway expansion underway for larger aircraft
  • Daily flights from major Pacific hub cities
  • Airport located 15 minutes from Taniti City

Coming Soon: The runway is being expanded to accommodate larger jets, which will increase flight options!

By Cruise Ship

Several cruise lines include Taniti as a port of call. Arrive in style and spend a day or more exploring our island.

  • Modern cruise port facilities
  • Walking distance to Taniti City
  • Shore excursions available for cruise passengers
  • Multiple cruise lines visit weekly during peak season

Getting Around the Island

Multiple transportation options to explore Taniti

Public Buses

Taniti's public bus system connects major areas of the island and is an affordable way to get around.

  • Routes connect Taniti City, beaches, and major attractions
  • Buses run frequently during daytime hours
  • Very affordable fares
  • Bus schedules available at hotels and tourist office

Note: Bus service is limited in the evenings. Plan accordingly!

Taxis

Private taxis are available throughout the island for convenient door-to-door service.

  • Available 24/7 in Taniti City
  • Can be hailed or booked through hotels
  • Fixed rates to common destinations
  • Island tours can be arranged with drivers

Rental Cars

For maximum flexibility, rent a car and explore the island at your own pace.

  • Several rental agencies near airport and in town
  • Valid driver's license required
  • Driving is on the RIGHT side of the road
  • Roads are well-maintained in populated areas
  • 4WD recommended for remote areas

Bicycles

Taniti's flat coastal areas are perfect for cycling. Bike rentals are available for eco-friendly exploration.

  • Rental shops in Taniti City and near beaches
  • Hourly, daily, and weekly rates available
  • Great for exploring coastal areas
  • Helmets included with rentals

Walking

Many areas of Taniti are very walkable, especially Taniti City and the beach areas.

  • Taniti City is compact and pedestrian-friendly
  • Beach promenades connect many areas
  • Most restaurants and shops within walking distance
  • Great way to discover hidden gems

Transportation Tips

Airport Transfers: Most hotels offer airport pickup. Book in advance!

Fuel: Gas stations are limited outside Taniti City. Fill up before exploring remote areas.

Rush Hour: Traffic can be heavy in Taniti City during morning and evening hours. Allow extra time.
